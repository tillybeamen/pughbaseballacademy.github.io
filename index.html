About Me

My name is Tillman Pugh, and I recently retired from baseball after over a decade of playing at various levels and around the world. I began my baseball journey at Arizona State University, the top-ranked college program in the country at the time, and was eventually drafted by the NY Mets in the 15th round of the 2010 MLB draft. I was overjoyed to fulfill my lifelong dream, but my professional debut was short-lived after a 95 mph pitch struck me in the face and required surgery. However, I persevered and returned to the field only five weeks later to finish the season.

After three years with the Mets, I was released, but I did not let this setback define me. I identified weaknesses in my game and sought help from a hitting coach to perfect my mechanics and mentality. Through this process, I learned that mastery takes time, perseverance, and a relentless pursuit of one's goals. Baseball is a game of constant adjustments, and it was through my dedication to improving my mental approach that I was able to overcome my struggles.

My career took another hit when I suffered a tear in my hip, but rather than giving up, I sought the help of a sports psychologist during my 3-5 month rehab. This decision was a turning point in my career, as it helped me develop the mental toughness needed to succeed in a sport where even the best players fail more often than they succeed.

In 2017, I had my best season yet, being named league MVP and winning my first professional championship. This success opened doors for me, and I was able to play in various leagues around the world, including in Japan and Mexico. Through these experiences, I learned the value of dedication, resilience, and continuous learning, which helped me go from making $300 a month to earning several times that in my last few years.

My story is one of perseverance, determination, and the pursuit of excellence. I am proud to have dedicated my career to baseball and to have honed my skills through hard work and a never-ending quest for knowledge. I am excited to share what I have learned with others and help them achieve their goals on and off the field.

Frequently Asked Questions

I train all ages

The equipment you bring depends on what your training goals are. If you are getting hitting lessons, make sure to bring a bat, batting gloves, and comfortable hitting shoes. If we are working on defense as well, make sure to bring your glove and a protective cup if you so choose. Most importantly, and regardless of trainging goals, always bring a great attitude!

I specialize in defensive work as well. In my over a decades worth of experience, I have played all three outfield positions as well as 2B at a high level. I can offer expert knowledge in baserunning fundamentals, arm care, diet and exercise, as well as mental preparation.
